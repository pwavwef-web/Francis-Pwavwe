WHO WE ARE

Built from real questions, real experiences.

Francis Pwavwe is a Tourism Management student at the University of Cape Coast, a Cadet Leader, and a storyteller who believes that the best content comes from people who have actually lived what they're talking about. That conviction is the foundation of everything produced under this name.

The journey started not with a plan, but with a question: why is it so hard to find content that both challenges you intellectually and feels genuinely human? Blue Mind Radio was the first answer — a podcast that invites young people to sit with ideas a little longer than the algorithm wants them to. Then came the essays, the video work, and eventually the productions brand.

"Every piece of content we make is a letter to someone who needed to hear exactly that — at exactly that moment."

Francis is also the founder of AZ Learner, an educational platform built to make learning accessible for students across Africa. The same values that drive AZ Learner — clarity, accessibility, impact — are at work in every Francis Pwavwe Production.

Cape Coast is not just a location on a map; it's a perspective. A place steeped in history, culture, and the tension between where Africa has been and where it's going. That perspective shapes every word written, every episode recorded, every frame shot.
